=== A MAJOR CHORD - ALL STEPS ===


STEP 1:
Understanding the A Major Chord: Here's a great memory hook - the A major chord looks like the number 1! A is the first letter of the alphabet, and 1 is the first number. The shape itself resembles that shape. You use three fingers on three adjacent strings, all on the same fret. The low E string (thickest) doesn't play - marked with X on the diagram.

---

STEP 2:
Finger Placement: Place your INDEX finger on 2nd fret of the D string (4th string). Place your MIDDLE finger on 2nd fret of the G string (3rd string). Place your RING finger on 2nd fret of the B string (2nd string). Keep your fingers curved and press down firmly. Pro tip: remember these three fingers are all on the same fret, in a straight line!

---

STEP 3:
Strumming: Here's the key rule - strum from the A string (5th string) downward. This is because A major is an "A-type" chord, so we start from the A string to get A as our lowest note. The lowest note you hear gives context to all the higher notes, so it determines the chord identity. Never hit the low E string on A major - it would make the chord sound wrong!

---

STEP 4:
Practice Tip: A major requires pressing hard and using good technique. You might not get a perfect sound immediately - that's normal! Focus on three things: (1) Press hard to avoid buzzing under your fingers. (2) Use the tips of your fingers to avoid muting the strings below. (3) Curve your fingers so they don't touch adjacent strings. Keep your wrist straight, not bent backward.
