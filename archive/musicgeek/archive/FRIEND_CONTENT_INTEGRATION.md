## Key Differences After Integration

### Example: A Major Lesson

**Before**:

> "The A major chord is one of the most beginner-friendly chords. You'll use three fingers on three adjacent strings, all on the same fret."

**After**:

> "Here's a great memory hook - the A major chord looks like the number 1! A is the first letter of the alphabet, and 1 is the first number. The shape itself resembles that shape. You use three fingers on three adjacent strings, all on the same fret."

Plus Step 3 now explains: "Strum from the A string because A major is an A-type chord, so we need A as our lowest note. The lowest note gives context to all the higher notes."

### Result

Students remember the shape faster AND understand WHY strumming choices matter, not just what to do.
